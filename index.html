DIVE INTO AN "ENDLESS SUMMER"
with Richard Mille
ABOUT PROJECT
“Endless Summer” by Richard Mille is a digital campaign created to promote the brand’s new watch collection – RM 07-01 Coloured Ceramics. This interactive experience is designed as a journey through an endless summer, where users move across vibrant, free-spirited, and joyful scenes throughout different moments of a summer day.

Using pastel tones such as pink, purple, and blue, combined with interactive techniques, “Endless Summer” delivers a storytelling experience that is both engaging and visually captivating. The brand transforms an advertising campaign into a true “digital art piece,” creating a space where users can experience an “endless summer” in their own way. Every scene and interaction cue is carefully crafted, artistic, and highly compelling.

Overall, through this interactive experience, Richard Mille conveys a freedom and enjoyment “luxury lifestyle” while showcasing limitless creativity in design, turning a watch into a work of art rather than just a timekeeping tool.

Experience "Endless Summer"
EXPERIENCE ANALYSIS
The first thing I noticed was the vibrant colors and art style of the website, but what impressed me most was the interactive instructions. The actions used throughout the experience are quite simple, but as a first-time user, I was surprised by how well they were combined with the animations and scenes.
- Reduce the browser size (on a PC or a laptop)
- Resize multiple times to see the animation
- Click the Start button
- Read the description
- Click Next repeatedly
- Enter scenarios (Archipelago, Beach, City)
- Drag and move the screen to explore
- Click special elements to reveal quotes
- Hold the screen to hear real-life sounds
- Zoom into points
- Turn the wheel to move timeline
- Complete all scenarios
- Click “Discover Your Journey”
I spent most of my time dragging and moving the screen to explore the interactive world.
My most common action in the two-minute interaction was turning the wheel to navigate through the timeline.

I think the primary goal of this website is clearly to market their new watch collection. However, it also aims to create a digital experience where users can relax, unwind, and reflect on the world in a colorful and meaningful way. Users can even leave the website open like a lo-fi music video.

This is a unique method of stress relief. It’s not just about reading a book with meaningful quotes; instead, you feel like you are inside the world being created, reading motivational quotes that are connected to each scene. You move through peaceful moments of life—at the beach, in the city, or in the archipelago—across different points in time. Simply wandering through the website makes me feel like I’m actually part of that calm world, living a relaxed life without worries. I really like how the artist transforms simple everyday moments into immersive scenarios where the audience can feel grateful and more motivated in life.

The interactive experience communicates the goal through:

- The action of reducing the browser size (on a PC or a laptop):
+ Using it on a mobile phone makes it easier to interact everywhere, every time + Create another experience for PC and laptop users so that they can see the animation when the browser is loaded, and reduce its size

- The scenes:
+ Beautiful art style
+ Illustrate real life, but in a positive and colorful way
+ Has some special elements that users can interact with + There are no detailed instructions; everything is in a general format (people, scene with no particular names) so it becomes everyone's own story

- The quotes:
+ Motivated quotes + Based on the scenes, it feels related when users explore the website

- The time wheel:
+ You can turn the wheel whenever you want + Create a 1-day-long experience

- The music:
+ Chill, lo-fi background music

- Real-life sound:
+ Make the experience realistic
In my opinion, this is an experience that should be revisited multiple times. Rather than limiting it to the three scenarios, users can explore as much as they want each time. It feels more like a never-ending experience, where you can move forward, go back to any stage, and revisit moments freely at your own pace.
It features three scenarios, each with eight time stamps, allowing users to experience the journey slowly and choose which moments they want to explore. Since the wheel can be turned both forward and backward, users have the freedom to navigate between scenes and revisit any moment they’re interested in.
It references music streaming platforms and radio (study, chill, lo-fi kind) as a background noise and image to look at when working, studying,... Also, it references storytelling through the timeline with visual and sound elements.
Since it is a radio and storytelling reference, I realise that I should interact slowly and actually dive into what is being communicated (with no stress or force to complete the experience). I should act like it is a chance for me to relax and escape from what I’m facing (deadlines, worrying thoughts, work,...). Moreover, I should pay attention to the details (not so strictly), just as much as I want (since it’s a personal experience), to understand deeply what the artist wants to communicate to the users.
Since it is a radio and storytelling reference and the website is covered with a chill and relaxing vibe, I should feel the same way. I should also feel curious (before entering the experience) about what will appear next, just like I am so curious, excited about what my life (near and far) will be in the future. In the moment of experiencing, I should feel no pressure and also feel willing to see what’s being told. After the experience, the feeling I should have is satisfied, grateful and impressed.

I think the part where you need to reduce the size of the browser is a bit frustrating. I tested on my iPad, and I need to turn on the Multitasking function to be able to reduce the size. And when I first opened it on my laptop, it took me a bit of time to understand the meaning behind that.

I think the reason why is that there are no instructions at the start, but reasonable because it’s a self-interactive experience; also, for aesthetic purposes, so I think the artist wants you to explore it and be amazed (I approved it!).
The part I find most satisfying is being able to press and hold the screen to hear the realistic sounds of the scene.

It’s so satisfying because it’s the most effective way to make the experience feel immersive and familiar. You really get the sense of being in the moment, which is relaxing. The sounds themselves are chill and ambient, making the whole interaction even more enjoyable.

THOUGHTS
“Endless Summer is a deep dive into a typical summer day. The season beckons you to embark on a vibrant journey, an immersion into an endless summer day. Allow yourself to wander as the sun ambles from East to West, and savour the luxury of a never-ending summer in a single, timeless experience.” (Richard Mille 2023)

What a memorable “Endless Summer” for me. This interactive experience combines visuals, sound, and a strong sense of satisfaction. At first, I thought it was a personal project by an artist or designer, but later I found out it is actually a marketing campaign for a new collection by Richard Mille, a watch brand. That’s when I understood why they use the wheel timeline as the main interaction - it represents how a watch works.

As the brand says, “a never-ending summer in a single, timeless experience,” this project really feels endless. You can move freely, go back and forth, and explore the story in your own way, making the whole experience feel continuous and timeless.

By Phuc An Nguyen
